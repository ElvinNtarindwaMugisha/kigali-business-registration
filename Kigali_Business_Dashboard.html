Key Findings

What the data tells us

Registrations grew 88% in 9 years (2015–2024) — from 13,180 to 24,800 nationally, with the only dip in 2016 (−15%). Post-2017 growth has been consistent, and 2024 posted the strongest year at +15.3%, driven by Rwanda's top-10 global B-READY ranking.
Wholesale & retail is dominant but hospitality is rising fast — at 54.6% of all establishments, retail remains king. But accommodation & food services at 23% reflects Kigali's emergence as a regional MICE hub, generating USD 84.8M in 2024 alone.
87% informality means official data undercounts the real economy — only ~1 in 8 businesses are formally registered. Micro enterprises (83.9% of all businesses) operate 93.7% informally, meaning Rwanda's true business formation rate is far higher than registration figures show.
Nyarugenge has the highest commercial density in Kigali — its density index of 0.32 (establishment share ÷ population share) makes it more commercially concentrated than Gasabo or Kicukiro, anchored by the CBD and formal financial services sector.
